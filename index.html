Byrek

Traditional savory pie with various fillings.

Tave Kosi

Baked lamb or veal with yogurt and rice.

Fergese

Peppers and tomatoes with cottage cheese or feta.

Speca me gjize

Roasted peppers with cheese filling.

Buke misri

Cornbread served with yogurt and cheese.

Petulla

Fried dough pastries, often served with powdered sugar or honey, and sometimes accompanied by jam or feta cheese.

Suxhuk

Spiced sausage often served as an appetizer.

Lakror

Layered pie with meat or vegetable fillings.

Japrak

Grape leaves stuffed with rice and herbs.

Pite

Various pies with different fillings.

Tarator

A refreshing and chilled yogurt-based soup, Tarator is a popular appetizer, especially during hot summer days. It includes yogurt, cucumbers, garlic, dill, and sometimes walnuts.

Sardele zgare

Fresh sardines marinated with olive oil, lemon, and herbs, then grilled to a smoky perfection.

Midhje te mbushura

Mussels filled with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes minced meat, creating a tasty and satisfying dish.

Karkaleca me domate dhe hudher

Shrimp cooked in a savory sauce made with tomatoes, garlic, and aromatic herbs.

Kallamare te mbushura

Calamari tubes filled with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes minced meat, then cooked to perfection.

Levrek i pjekur

Sea bass marinated and grilled, often served with a side of fresh vegetables or a light salad.

Oktapod i pjekur

Octopus marinated in olive oil, garlic, and herbs, then baked to perfection. It's a flavorful and tender seafood dish.

Flija

Layered pancake often served with honey.

Tave Dheu

Slow-cooked lamb or goat with vegetables.

Fasule me mish

Stewed meat and beans.

Bakllava

Layers of phyllo dough filled with chopped nuts (often walnuts or almonds) and sweetened with honey or sugar syrup.

Hasude

A dessert with water, butter, caramelized sugar, starch and nuts.

Revani

A semolina cake soaked in a simple syrup made with sugar, water, and lemon juice. Revani is often flavored with vanilla and garnished with almonds or coconut.

Ballokume

A festive cookie often prepared during holidays. It's a buttery cookie made with flour, sugar, and eggs, and it can be flavored with lemon or orange zest.

Trilece

A creamy and caramel-flavored dessert made with sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and regular milk. It's often topped with a layer of caramel sauce.

Ashure

A dessert that is prepared with boiled wheat, starch, figs and raisins, a little buckwheat and bran, crushed nuts on top, being sweetened with sugar or honey, but also with cider in the absence of these.

Hallva

Halva is a delectable dessert with origins in the Middle East, featuring a sweet, dense, and crumbly confection made from ground sesame seeds or other nut butters, often blended with sugar and spices for a rich and flavorful treat.

Qofte te ferguara

Fried meatballs, often served during celebrations.

Qifqi

Rice or bulgur balls stuffed with minced meat.

Tave kosi me mish

Baked lamb or veal with stuffed meat.

Raki

Traditional Albanian brandy from grape juice.

Boze

Fermented beverage made from maize or wheat.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here you can get information about the most frequent questions about Albania from tourists

1. What defines Albanian cuisine?

Albanian cuisine is characterized by a rich tapestry of flavors, influenced by Mediterranean and Balkan traditions. It features an abundance of fresh produce, meats, dairy, and unique spices that contribute to its diverse and hearty dishes.

2. What are some must-try Albanian dishes?

Explore the flavors of Albanian cuisine with iconic dishes such as Byrek (savory pie), Tave Kosi (baked lamb with yogurt), Fergese (peppers and tomatoes with cheese), and traditional desserts like Bakllava and Trilece.

3. Are the recipes on this site authentic?

Our recipes are inspired by traditional Albanian dishes and are crafted to capture the essence of the cuisine. While we aim for authenticity, some variations may exist due to personal preferences or regional nuances.

4. How can I adapt recipes to suit dietary preferences or restrictions?

Our recipes are versatile, and many can be adjusted to accommodate dietary preferences or restrictions. Check the recipe notes for suggested modifications or reach out to our community for personalized tips.

5. Is there a section for traditional Albanian beverages?

Yes, explore our "Beverages" category for traditional Albanian drinks such as Raki and Boze. You'll find refreshing options that complement the richness of Albanian cuisine.

6. How can I stay connected with the Albanian culinary community?

Join our newsletter for updates, follow us on social media for vibrant discussions, and participate in our forums to connect with fellow Albanian cuisine enthusiasts. Your culinary journey is shared and celebrated within our community.
